Takeaway

Content discovery difficulty

2. Content overload makes interaction complex (Hick's law).
3. Information display does not match user's metal model.
4. Low discoverability of article entry point results in low view rate.

II. Home Page

Easy and engaging interaction of exploring daily suggested content

I explored several solutions to address the problem of content exploring difficulty, and validated the design through various usability test methods.


Motivate Users to Make Experience Engaging



How to motivate users to explore the contents?
My solution came from the inspiration of the college student learning management system dashboard, which every college student would check on every day.

I redefined the experience to be a dashboard of users' learning status, like the school assignment dashboard every college student would check on every day.
We looked into several school dashboards to gather inspiration, and found out the key elements that a dashboard should contain to be motivating are:

List of tasks

Progress


The ideation process on reflecting the key elements into the wireframe went through divergence and convergence cycles. The goal was to give users a overview of their status, and display as much as contents with minimum interaction cost.

Reflections

Don‚Äôt frame yourself inside a box. Always dig under the surface.

In the very beginning of the design sprint, I was bounded by the context given, and got the assumption that the user flows must be poor in result of bad content view rate. However, assumptions never fully uncover reasons for user's behavior. By digging behind data and listening to the users, product problems were framed from real use scenarios.

What could we do better?

We were not able to gather more data of user's behavior in the very beginning. A throughout business analysis could inform the decision better. If I could work more closely with the PM and business team of Banmi, the design approach could have been targeted towards the goal better.
