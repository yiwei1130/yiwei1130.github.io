Background

About VeeR

VeeR is a leading VR entertainment platform available in 180+ countries, across all major VR headsets, web, and mobile. With over 20M downloads, now it has grown to be one of the most popular VR content community platform worldwide.

VeeR was founded in late 2016, the MVP was launched in Nov. 2016. This project was intended to uncover its usability problems and to provide insights for VeeR's future product plan.

Challenge

VR video was a relatively new form of media in 2016 and not many people had experience on it.
As a mobile app aiming to create a platform of VR video sharing, VR mode is the most important watching mode.  

To open up the market and gain more audience, the biggest problem VeeR confront was:

The VR watching mode use rate is low, compared to the size of its user base.

Frame the problem

Product Reviews

Soon after VeeR's launch in US Apple Store, it quickly attracted good amount of users. The product team gathered a lot of good feedback on the concept of the App, but in the meantime tons of complaints on the usability.


To find out the problem and  increase VeeR's usability, I worked as a consultant to lead a usability testing to identify usability problems and find out opportunities to help VeeR's localization in US market. 9 participants were recruited to gather valuable insights.

During the Usability Testing

Empathize With Users

During the usability testing, I found out there were two main user drop-off points in the user flow to watch a video in VR mode.

User Flow Of VR ideo Watching - Drop-off Point

Drop-off Point 1: Users don't know how to switch to VR mode

Flow of switching from mobile mode to VR mode

User Feedback

“ I thought there was only a mobile mode! It's so hard to notice the VR mode button.”
“ This task is hard, and I felt very overwhelmed not knowing how I should interact with the app”

Drop-off Point 2: Setting-up Process Disrupted Video Watching Experience

From the results of the follow-up questionnaire, 6/9 users expressed they feel the process of docking the phone into the headset interrupted the video watching experience. While they were setting up the headset, the video was still playing, as a result part of the video would be missed.

While user setting up the headset, video was playing

User Feedback

“It made me feel very nervous and I was afraid I did it too slowly.”

Challenge

How could we
boost first-time user's confidence and relief the anxiety for setting up VR headset?

design approach

I started to brainstorm concepts to boost user's confidence, and relief the anxiety.

IMPACT

VeeR used our research insights to inform their development plan. My design suggestions were incorporated into VeeR's later versions. Now VeeR has reached to over 20M users with over 20M downloads, in over 180 countries.

After the onboarding flow was launched, the VR watching mode use rate was bumped by 56%.

Reflections

User reactions vary dramatically from person to person. There will always be situations we never imagined, and users will least likely behave in the way we wanted them to. Design can never cover all the scenarios, but as designers we should add tolerance and flexibility in our design. We can't expect users get everything right all the time. Errors should be tolerable and easy and relaxed feedback should be given to maintain the confidence of users.

Making the participants feel confident and relaxed during user testing. Users tend to feel themselves as students taking exams, they are afraid to make mistake.

Easy and casual experiences will help open up tester's mouthes, and they will behave more like they really do in their real life. It will help uncover the problems we are digging for.
