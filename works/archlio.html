background

What are submittals?

Submittals are drawings and documents that a contractor uses in the building process. They are provided by manufacturers and need to be reviewed and approved by architects.

Two most headache-causing facts about the submittal review process:

  • There are tons of them!!
  • Hard to coordinate co-review process: each submittal needs several rounds of review. Each round involves people in different roles.

Missing a due can be bad, while missing dues is common...

According an internal survey in the company I work for, due missing happens as often as 72%.
Architects can be sued and subject to huge fines because of missing submittal review dues.

Frame the problem

A submittal's lifecycle

Submittal reviewing is a long, redundant process that involves a lot of stakeholders, architects mainly involve in the Review and Return phases of submittal review.

What is killing the efficiency of architects?

To understand current problems that cause due missing, I conducted a user survey among 25 of architects. I mapped out the journey of architects' reviewing process, and found three main pain points that are killing the efficiency.

Pain points

  • Unclear of each submittal's due, lack of information to prioritize work accordingly
  • Current method of due reminding (conversation, emails, paper reports) is labor-heavy and not effective
  • Delivering on time needs team efforts, but co-reviewing process is hard to coordinate.

design process

Establishing design goals

  • Improve the clarity of submittals' progress & due dates, help users be aware of job progress
  • Provide efficient in-time due reminders to help users make work plan
  • Simplify the co-review coordination experience between consultants and architects

What do architects really need to see in the dashboard to know one submittal's status?

Survey Result

Justifying Design Details  – Intuitive & Interactive Flow for Task & Notification

How do Task and Notification help simplify architect's workflow? I iterated my design solution with insights I gathered from user test.

Iteration 1

Insights from usability testing with 6 users:

  • Notifications of only numbers don't give users much contextual information about which submittals are due soon.
  • Lack of information to build a connection between these notifications and the work needs to be prioritized.
Iteration 2

Insights From usability testing with 6 users:

  • Being able to see submittal lists at notification tab is helpful, and the flow to open submittal details is intuitive and efficient.
  • Manually input task item is tedious.
  • Task lists don't give straightforward references of which ones are the tasks about, so it is not really helping the workflow.
Final Iteration

With the insights from previous iterations, I synthesize the user flow with the task and notification into the following:

I focused on improve the interactivity and flexibility of the creating task flow and review notifications flow. By allowing directly adding notification items into task list, users are able to manage tasks more easily, and keep track of items that are due soon, and prioritize work with minimum effort.

Final Design

Highlights

  • Create tasks directly from notification items.
  • Tasks with links easily take users to the related items.

Goal III
How to make co-reviewing process easier?


Coordination is one of the biggest headache-causing part of architects' workflow.
‍How to simplify the back and forth process of co-reviewing between architects and consultants?

Problem: Coordination is hard...

My solution


My solution is to integrate the job distributing process and reminding consultants to return submittals process into Archlio. By avoiding switching between platforms (verbally, emails, meetings), the process is consistent and streamlined.

Step 1. User Flow
Justifying Design Details  – Filters

What kind of filters best help to simplify the flow? I explored two different designs and tested them with 6 users.

Design 1 - Modal

Pros:

  • Easy flow

Cons:

  • The button can be hard to find for some users.
  • When adding filters in a modal dialog, the results showing in the dashboard won't change until user apply all the filters they add. Not real-time results will probably result in extra interaction cost.
  • There is higher chance in error input. Users won't know which filter they added is not correct when they get undesired results.
Design 2  – Interactive Sheet Header Filter

Wins:

  • Intuitive interaction
  • Instant results for each filter applied
  • Better control on each filter.
Testing Summary

I developed filter interaction schemes for each column's header.

Final Design

By incorporating the dynamic and responsive filters, distributing work and sending reminders become simple and efficient in only one platform.

Reflections

A User-Feedback Driven Design Process

Designing for a platform for architect, I naturally take the advantage of my own experience. However, for every SaaS platform like this one, every single user's experience can be different. And it is important to gain deeper insights by learning from all the edge cases.
I tested with my solutions extensively, from small UI item, micro-interaction, to the flow of a feature. Collecting user feedback constantly not only helped me removed any blocker on the spot, but also deepen my understanding of user behaviors.
